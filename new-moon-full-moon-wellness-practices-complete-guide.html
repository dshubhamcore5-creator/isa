New Moon and Full Moon Wellness Practices:
A Complete Guide

By Aditi - Spiritual Practitioner · Infinite Soul Awakening Wellness 16 min read

Wellness practices rooted in intention — rituals, crystal work, energetic alignment, guided release — are not reserved for special occasions. At Infinite Soul Awakening Wellness, these practices are woven into daily life, adapted to whatever the day, the season, and the individual's circumstances call for. But certain days carry a heightened energetic quality that makes the same practices land more deeply.

Wellness Practices Are a Daily Commitment — Lunar Days Amplify Them

Before going further, it is worth being clear about something that distinguishes a genuine wellness practice from a superficial one. Intention-setting, energetic alignment, crystal work, guided release, and reflective wellness practices are not things that happen only on the full moon or the new moon. They are daily orientations — ways of moving through life with awareness, purpose, and attention to what is being cultivated and what is being released.

What makes New Moon and Full Moon days different is not that wellness practices suddenly become possible — it is that the energetic conditions on these days are considered particularly auspicious. The same practice performed on a new moon or full moon carries additional momentum. Think of it like planting seeds: you can plant any day, but a skilled gardener also pays attention to the season and timing because timing amplifies outcomes.

Why Are Amavasya and Purnima Considered Auspicious?

The relationship between the moon and human life is one of the oldest and most universally shared frameworks in human history — observed by ancient Indian traditions including Vedic astrology and the Hindu calendar, by Chinese and Tibetan medicine, by Celtic and Norse traditions, and by Indigenous cultures across every continent.

In Vedic trAditi - Spiritual Practitioneron specifically — deeply embedded in the cultural fabric of India — the lunar calendar governs religious observance, auspicious timing, and sacred practice. Ekadashi, Purnima, Amavasya — these are lived rhythms that have shaped how millions of people mark time and begin endeavours for thousands of years.

Amavasya — New Moon

Planting Seeds & Setting Intentions

The moment of greatest darkness and deepest inward potential. The most auspicious time for new beginnings, intention-setting, and clearing old energy to make space for the new.

Purnima — Full Moon

Release, Completion & Renewal

The moment of peak illumination. The most auspicious time for releasing what no longer serves, completing cycles, emotional clearing, and energetic renewal.

Full Moon Renewal Sessions are the energetic mirror of New Moon sessions, oriented toward release and renewal:

  • Candle wellness practice — black candles for energetic release, or gold and silver for illumination and completion
  • Energetic clearing — clearing accumulated heaviness to restore a felt sense of lightness and personal clarity

Other Auspicious Days for Wellness Practices

  • Ekadashi The 11th day of each lunar fortnight, regarded in Vedic trAditi - Spiritual Practitioneron as one of the most spiritually potent days of the month for clarity and inner receptivity.
  • Seasonal Transitions Winter solstice, spring equinox, summer solstice, and autumn equinox mark profound shifts in natural energy.
  • Personal Dates Birthdays, anniversaries, and personally significant dates carry heightened intentional energy for practices around new beginnings.
  • Any Day of Need The most auspicious day for a wellness practice is always the day you genuinely need one.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a New Moon wellness session?
A New Moon wellness session is a personalised online wellness experience designed around intention-setting, new beginnings, and planting seeds for the cycle ahead. It may include intention-setting guidance, crystal alignment, candle wellness practice, and a written intention framework.
What is a Full Moon wellness session?
A Full Moon wellness session focuses on release, completion, emotional clearing, and renewal. It may include guided release practice, crystal alignment, candle wellness practice, and energetic clearing.
Why are Amavasya and Purnima considered auspicious?
Amavasya and Purnima are significant points in the lunar cycle in Indian and Vedic traditions. Amavasya is associated with inward potential and new beginnings, while Purnima is associated with illumination, completion, and release.
Are wellness practices only done on lunar dates?
No. Wellness practices can be meaningful on any day of genuine personal need. New Moon and Full Moon days are considered amplifiers, not the only times when practice is possible.
Can I book lunar wellness sessions online from India?
Yes. New Moon, Full Moon, Ekadashi, and seasonal wellness sessions are available online across India and worldwide.
