What Are Runes?

Runes are an ancient set of symbols originating from the Germanic and Nordic traditions of Northern Europe, used for thousands of years as both a writing system and a symbolic framework for reflection, divination, and personal guidance. The word "rune" itself derives from an Old Norse root meaning secret or mystery — reflecting the depth of meaning attributed to each symbol.

The most widely used rune system today is the Elder Futhark — a set of 24 symbols each carrying a distinct archetypal meaning. Unlike a modern alphabet, each rune is not simply a letter — it is a symbol associated with natural forces, human experiences, and universal life themes.

Where Do Runes Come From?

Runes have been in use across Northern Europe for at least 2,000 years, with the earliest runic inscriptions dating to approximately the 2nd century CE. They were used by Germanic and Norse peoples across Scandinavia, the British Isles, and Central Europe — appearing on stones, weapons, jewellery, and ceremonial objects.

What Kinds of Questions Are Runes Best Suited to Explore?

Rune guidance tends to be particularly powerful for:

  • Personal transitions and change — career shifts, relationship changes, relocations, or significant life decisions
  • Strength and resilience — illuminating personal strengths and offering perspective during difficult periods
  • Inner direction — reconnecting with your own inner compass when the path ahead feels unclear
  • Energetic patterns and life cycles — moments of ending and beginning, where old chapters close and new ones have not yet formed
  • Relationship dynamics — the archetypal patterns at play between people and the inner work each person brings to a connection
